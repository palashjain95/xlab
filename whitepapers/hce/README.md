# The Human Context Economy — Executive Summary

**Why this paper matters**
Every industrial revolution commoditised the previous era's scarce resource. AI is now commoditising general reasoning—summarisation, pattern-matching, boiler-plate code—at scale and near-zero cost. What remains scarce is *human context*: the situated expertise, tacit judgement, and lived experience that models cannot replicate. This paper maps the emerging economy that forms around that scarcity.

---

## Core Thesis
As AI makes generic reasoning abundant, niche human context becomes the scarce economic fuel. The value stack flips: standardised cognitive tasks trend toward commodity pricing, while expert nuance and proprietary lived data command a premium.

---

## Key Insights
| Pain Point | Evidence |
|---|---|
| **Commoditised reasoning** | AI models perform summarisation, classification, and code generation at human-competitive levels; marginal cost → zero. |
| **Unmonetised expertise** | Domain experts lack scalable channels to package, price, and distribute knowledge into AI workflows. |
| **Trust & IP gap** | No standard way to verify credentials, audit provenance, or prevent zero-cost copying of high-value prompts. |

---

## The Opportunity Layers (Six-Layer Framework)

1. **Capture & Credential** — Verifiable, living proof-of-expertise (skill passports, passive capture plug-ins) that underpins trust in every downstream layer.
2. **Context DevOps** — Git-style versioning, testing, and safe rollback for prompts, embeddings, and knowledge capsules.
3. **Routing & Orchestration** — Real-time matching of the right expert capsule to the right agent call, with per-call billing and compliance support.
4. **Monetisation & Rights Mgmt** — Streaming royalties and DRM for context-token usage, solving the "prompt leak" problem.
5. **Worker UX & Tooling** — No-code capsule builders and context wallets that make participation as frictionless as posting on Substack.
6. **Upskilling & Discovery** — Skill-gap analysers fed by agent error logs, auto-generating learning paths and credentials to close the reskilling loop.

---

## Broader Implications
**Policy:** New classifications and protections for "context workers"; licensing frameworks for metered knowledge usage.
**Labour:** Further shift toward freelance micro-contributions; tooling and upskilling are load-bearing pillars, not afterthoughts.
**Investment:** Each layer is a greenfield startup playground—from credential infrastructure to royalty engines.
