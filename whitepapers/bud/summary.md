# B.U.D — Bharat Universal DevOps — Executive Summary

**Why this paper matters**
India gave every citizen a digital identity (Aadhaar) and every merchant a payment channel (UPI). The missing layer in this Digital Public Architecture is *deployment*—the infrastructure that turns code into live, scalable products. B.U.D proposes a publicly funded, open DevOps platform that makes cloud deployment as accessible as sending a UPI payment.

---

## Core Thesis
Universal DevOps will significantly reduce the go-to-market time for new digital products, empower innovation by lowering technical and financial barriers, and promote digital sovereignty by fostering homegrown solutions.

---

## Key Insights
| Pain Point | Evidence |
|---|---|
| **Prohibitive cost** | A small cloud environment costs ₹8k–₹20k/month; for every \$1 on infra, \$2.50 more goes to DevOps management (Forrester). |
| **Skills gap** | 1.5M engineers graduate annually, yet 80% lack practical deployment skills (Aspiring Minds). |
| **Stifled innovation** | 90% of Indian startups fail within 5 years; infrastructure complexity is a significant contributor. |
| **Rural exclusion** | 50%+ of rural students have limited exposure to hands-on cloud tools (NASSCOM). |

---

## Design Principles
Zero-cost base tier, one-click deploy, open & interoperable standards, federated hosting across state data centres and universities, Aadhaar/DigiLocker–linked identity, and first-class AI/ML support.

---

## The DPA Precedent
Aadhaar (1.2B enrolments, launched 2009) and UPI (10B+ txns/month, launched 2016) prove that publicly funded digital rails catalyse private-sector innovation far beyond the initial investment. B.U.D applies the same playbook to cloud deployment.

---

## Impact Target
Deploy time from weeks → minutes. Student project cost from ₹8k–₹20k/month → ₹0. Graduate deployment experience from ~20% → 60%+ within five years. ~40% IT infrastructure cost reduction (Gartner benchmark).
